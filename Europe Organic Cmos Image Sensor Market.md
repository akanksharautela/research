Definition of Organic CMOS Image Sensor Market
The Organic CMOS Image Sensor market is defined by products that utilize organic photoconductive films layered over CMOS backplanes. These components offer advanced imaging capabilities, including enhanced sensitivity to light, wider dynamic range, and improved performance under low-light conditions. The market comprises key components such as organic photodiodes, readout circuits, and supporting technologies for integration into end-user systems.

Key terms related to this market include:

Organic Photoconductive Film: A critical component enabling light absorption and conversion into electronic signals.

Dynamic Range: The range of light intensities that sensors can capture, from the darkest to the brightest areas.

Quantum Efficiency: The effectiveness of a sensor in converting incoming light into usable electrical signals.
